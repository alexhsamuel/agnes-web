About Your Plant

Sansevieria trifasciata is a stemless, evergreen perennial that can last many years. This resilient plant has thick, pointed leaves that are quite striking. It can handle low to bright light, requires very little water, and filters nasty stuff out of the air.

This plant is native to west Africa, and can grow to as much as 4’ tall in the wild. As an indoor plant it takes its time growing and generally maxes out at 2’.

The Sansevieria genus has over 70 varieties, though roughly 15 varieties are commonly found as house plants.

Plant Care

Light

Low to bright light. This plant tolerates low-light situations better than intense, direct sunlight.

Water

When soil is dry to touch, water the dirt directly and avoid getting any trapped within the rosettes of the leaves since that can lead to rot. Time between watering depends on the environment, and can be from 1-2+ weeks.

Use distilled water, or just leave a jug of tap water out for 48 hours so things like chlorine and fluoride dissipate.

Overwatering is the biggest problem with this plant!

Dirt

This plant likes being tight in its pot, so you don’t have to worry about repotting often. Go a level up when roots poke out of the dirt, and use aerated soil like cactus or succulent mix. Avoid soils with excess nitrogen, like Miracle Gro.

Rocks at the base of the pot (before you top with soil) are a good idea. We like lava.

Propagation

Here are 3 ways to propagate sansevieria:

  1. Splitting — Literally split 1 plant into 2 plants using a sharp knife at the roots. This method maintains the color variation of the plant. Place in different pots.
  2. Rhizome — This plant produces “baby” versions of itself that spread from the main plant. You can split these mini versions off by removing the plant from the pot and taking a sharp knife to the “rhizome,” which looks like a fat root that curves up. Replant in separate pots. This method maintains the color variation of the plant.
  3. Leaf cutting — Cut a healthy leaf at its base, then cut it into 3 inch sections (or bigger). Let the sections “heal” for 1-2 days, and place directly into well-aerated dirt (again, cactus/succulent mix + lava rocks). You can also place these into a cup of water, and watch as roots form over the next month or more. Be sure to keep the cuttings facing “up” — you put them in water or dirt upside down, they will not root. You’ll lose color variation with this method.

Flowers

This plant very rarely flowers indoors, but if it does, you’ll see fragrant white sprays.

Oh no!

Squishy, yellow leaves? Overwatered.

Brown, wrinkled leaves? Underwatered.

Pets

Mildly toxic to pets who like eating plants, particularly cats. Effects can include minor irritation but can potentially cause nausea or diarrhea depending on how much is ingested. If you are a pet owner, please do your research before bringing a sansevieria or any other plant home!
